Assessment Strategy Guide

  • Formative Assessment: Use to check understanding during learning (quizzes, exit tickets, discussions)
  • Summative Assessment: Use to evaluate learning at end of unit (final exams, projects, presentations)
  • Performance-Based: Students demonstrate skills in authentic contexts (portfolio, case study, project)
  • Adaptive Assessment: AI adjusts difficulty based on student responses
  • Peer Assessment: Students evaluate each other with AI-generated rubrics

Research Writing Checklist

  • ✓ Clear thesis statement aligned with research question
  • ✓ Literature review cites 15+ recent sources
  • ✓ Methodology section is reproducible
  • ✓ Findings presented without bias or interpretation
  • ✓ Discussion connects findings to literature
  • ✓ Proper citations (APA/MLA/Chicago) throughout
  • ✓ Grammar and spelling checked
  • ✓ Tables and figures are clear and labeled
  • ✓ Conclusion summarizes main contributions
  • ✓ Potential limitations acknowledged

Assessment Prompts

1. Generate Multiple Choice Questions

"Create 10 multiple-choice questions on [TOPIC] for [GRADE LEVEL] students. Questions should range from basic (definitions) to intermediate (application). For each question provide: - Question stem - 4 answer options (A-D) - Correct answer - Brief explanation of why correct answer is right - Note on common misconception addressed (if applicable) Make questions aligned with [LEARNING STANDARD if applicable]."

2. Create Assessment Rubric

"Create a detailed 4-point rubric for assessing [ASSIGNMENT TYPE] on [TOPIC] for [GRADE LEVEL] students. Include these criteria: [LIST SPECIFIC CRITERIA] For each criterion, provide: - Excellent (4): Clear descriptor - Good (3): Clear descriptor - Fair (2): Clear descriptor - Poor (1): Clear descriptor Make descriptors specific, observable, and measurable."

3. Design Performance Task

"Design a performance task for [GRADE LEVEL] students to demonstrate understanding of [LEARNING OBJECTIVE]. The task should: - Be authentic and real-world relevant - Take approximately [TIME] to complete - Require [SPECIFY SKILLS/KNOWLEDGE] - Produce a tangible product or performance Include: - Clear instructions - Success criteria - Evaluation rubric - Extensions for advanced learners"

Research & Writing Prompts

1. Develop Research Question

"Help me develop research questions for a paper on [TOPIC]. Research context: - Target audience: [AUDIENCE] - Scope: [GEOGRAPHIC/TEMPORAL SCOPE] - Existing knowledge: [WHAT'S ALREADY KNOWN] - Research gap: [WHAT NEEDS TO BE STUDIED] Suggest 5 progressively complex research questions that would be suitable for a [PAPER TYPE] paper."

2. Analyze Literature

"Analyze the following research findings on [TOPIC]: [INSERT ABSTRACT OR KEY POINTS] Provide: 1. Main argument/contribution 2. Methodology (briefly) 3. Key findings 4. Limitations 5. How this relates to [YOUR BROADER RESEARCH QUESTION] 6. Potential gaps this research leaves open"

3. Improve Academic Writing

"Review this paragraph from my [PAPER SECTION] and suggest improvements: [INSERT PARAGRAPH] Please provide: 1. Clarity: Is it easy to understand? Any jargon that needs explanation? 2. Academic tone: Does it match formal academic writing? 3. Sentence structure: Any run-ons or fragments? 4. Evidence: Does it cite sources where needed? 5. Conciseness: Any redundant phrases? Provide specific suggestions for improvement."

4. Generate Outline

"Create a detailed outline for a [PAPER TYPE] paper on [TOPIC]. Paper scope: - Target length: [WORD COUNT] - Target audience: [AUDIENCE] - Main argument: [YOUR THESIS] - Key points to cover: [LIST MAIN POINTS] Provide a hierarchical outline with: - Main sections - Subsections with 2-3 bullet points for each - Suggested citations per section (approximate)"

Pro Tips for Using Prompts

  • Be Specific: More detail in your prompt = better output
  • Iterate: First output isn't final. Ask for refinements
  • Customize: Adapt templates to your specific needs
  • Review Always: Fact-check and ensure accuracy before using with students
  • Combine Tools: Use ChatGPT for content, Grammarly for editing, Zotero for citations
